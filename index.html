Hello!

My name is Jennifer Allen. I am a native of Wyoming, born and raised in Rock Springs, Wyoming. I have lived in the great state of Wyoming almost my entire life. In 1996, I married my high-school sweetheart and in 1997 we moved to Laramie, Wyoming, to continue our college educations. In 2003, my husband and I moved to Casper, Wyoming, and were blessed with a baby boy. In 2004, we moved back to Rock Springs where we hung our hats until this past March, 2026, when we uprooted our lives and moved to Texas. What a new adventure it has been so far!

After high school, I attended Western Wyoming Community College in Rock Springs where I received my AAS in Office Information Systems in 1993. I continued my education at Western until I relocated to Laramie to attend the University of Wyoming. In 1999, I received my BS in Communications & Mass Media (minoring in graphic design, marketing, and business administration). I continued my education at the University of Wyoming receiving my MS in e.Business in 2002.

In 1995, I began my journey as a professional freelance graphic designer and web designer. In 2002 after graduating with my master's degree in e.Business, I added other services to my offerings including web development, computer application installation, setup, and deployment, and a wide variety of other computer support services. Additionally, I started a line of custom candy bar wrappers and bottle labels called “Occasional Labels” by Angel Designs, a brand I created with the play on my maiden name of Angelovic. While this professional freelance work has not always been a full-time endeavor, I have spent many hours completing work as a professional freelancer and still do to this day in addition to my full-time faculty profession. While I am excited about my new adventures in Texax, I am sad to say that I will no longer be a full-time faculty member at Western. I have accepted a full-time faculty position at Sam Houston State University as a Lecturer of Information Systems in the College of Business Administration. I am very excited to start this new position this fall!

During my time as a full-time faculty member at Western, I got to teach courses including Computer Information Systems, Introduction to Information Management, Information Management and Security, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Rush, Microsoft Word, Microsoft Excel, Microsoft PowerPoint, Microsoft Access, Microsoft Outlook, Web Development and Design, eCommerce, IT Fundamentals, ERP Systems, Digital Video Design, and various other courses as needed. I also got to do some other awesome things like advise students, promote our progams, go to conferences, and attend fun campus events. I am so incredibly grateful for the opportunities afforded me at Western as they have helped me grow and learn and become the instructor I am today. If it weren't for these experiences, I would not have been able to get a teaching position at a university. I do look forward to staying in touch with Western as an adjunct instructor whenever I can. It has been such a huge part of my life, it is hard to give it up completely. Thanks, Western!
